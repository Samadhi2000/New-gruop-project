diyaluma falls





GO, SEE, DO-IT-HARD, WATERFALL.
After viewing Diyaluma, the second-tallest and most spectacular waterfall in Sri Lanka, we would advise you to do that.
Diyaluma Falls, which can be reached from Ella in approximately one hour, is situated in the highlands of Sri Lanka,
offering breathtaking vistas and some of the world's greatest natural infinity pools.
First glimpses of the higher falls are seen after a two-hour tuk tuk journey from Ella to Poonagala through characteristically lush Sri Lankan tea estates,
followed by a 30-minute trek downwards amid long, dry grasses.
After a short climb, you'll find yourself standing on what looks to be the edge of the earth,
overlooking a valley that descends 220 meters below the flowing water.
We cherish life a little too much, so regrettably,
we were unable to swim in the lower Diyaluma Falls pools because we were there during the rainy season.
As we stood at the top of the falls, taking in the sun setting over the valley below,
some young local boys eagerly joined us to discuss our trips and, of course, cricket.
After taking a few drone flights, relaxing, and taking some pictures, we reluctantly started the trip back to Ella.
It was one of our favorite things to do in Sri Lanka and the ideal way to spend a Sunday afternoon from Ella.
We've included all the information you need in our comprehensive guide to Diyaluma so you can make the most of your stay here.
You can take a cool swim in any of the natural pools, including the one directly beside the main waterfall,
in the summer when the water levels have decreased. It's worth taking a cold plunge; it's essentially the world's nicest infinity pool.
As an alternative, the upper Diyaluma falls provide several larger, safer pools; still, proceed with caution before entering.
